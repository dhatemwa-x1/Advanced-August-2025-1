The Three Acts of a Story

A single picture is just Act 2. A great storyteller imagines all three.

⬅️

Act 1: The Past

What events led to this exact moment?

▶️

Act 2: The Present

What is happening right now, and what does it feel like?

➡️

Act 3: The Future

What will happen immediately after this moment?

Grammar for Act 1: The Past

Let's build a rich and detailed backstory.

Category: Modal Verbs of Deduction

must have"The owner must have been a traveler."
can't have"This chest can't have been opened for decades."
might have"They might have hidden it here to keep it safe."

Category: Cleft Sentences for Emphasis

It was... that..."It was the compass, not the map, that first caught my eye."
What... was..."What intrigued me most was the destination on the map."

Category: Speculating on Past Habits

would + verb"Every summer, the grandfather would climb to the attic."
used to + verb"This attic used to be a child's secret hideout."

Grammar for Act 2: The Present

Describing the scene with advanced clauses.

Category: Participle Clauses

-ing clause"Pointing towards an unknown land, the compass invites a journey."
-ed clause"Hidden under a white sheet, an old armchair waits in the corner."

Category: Adjectives & Unreal Comparisons

Adjectives"The single beam of light creates a poignant and nostalgic atmosphere."
as if / as though"The dust motes dance in the light as if they are tiny ghosts."

Grammar for Act 3: The Future

Using advanced tenses to predict what's next.

Category: Future Perfect & Continuous

Future Perfect"By next year, they will have discovered what the map leads to."
Future Continuous"Soon, they will be sailing across the ocean, following this map."

Category: Future in the Past

was/were about to"Their ordinary lives were about to change forever."
was/were going to"This compass was going to lead him to his destiny."

Category: Conditionals for Possibilities

First Conditional"If they follow that map, they will find the treasure."
Second Conditional"If I found a chest like this, I would keep it a secret."
Third Conditional"If they had left the chest closed, they would have missed the adventure."

Your Task: The Storytellers' Workshop

1. Get Your Photo: In your groups, you will receive a secret photograph.

2. Plan (10 mins): Use the grammar we've reviewed to discuss and plan the Past, Present, and Future of your photo.

3. Write (30 mins): Write one complete story that weaves together all three acts. Try to use a variety of the new grammar structures.

Your goal is to use this grammar to create the most interesting and mysterious story in the class!

Good Luck!

Let the storytelling begin.
